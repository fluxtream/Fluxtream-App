Team
BodyTrack consists of a number of open-source components. We currently have a team of around 10 contributors to the BodyTrack open source projects. We're at a fairly exciting time in the development lifecycle: many things are coming to life! If you'd like to consider contributing, please contact Anne Wright (anne@bodytrack.org). Please include information about your interests and skills, and how best to reach you.
Open-Source projects in BodyTrack
BodyTrack website (ROR)
Very active development; currently in alpha test
Explorable data viewer (Javascript/Canvas/GWT)
Interactive exploration of very large datasets (pulls in data on the fly as user zooms and explores)
Current featureset works well; very active development; currently in alpha test
Fast datastore (C++)
Scales to 1TB per channel; response time < 10 msec for snippets at any level of detail, even summarizing entire 1TB
In process of deploying to alpha website to replace MySQL-based datastore
Android app (Java) [github repository]
Records accelerometry, gyros, GPS (with access-point optimizations), pictures, and comments/tags In alpha test
iPhone app
Not yet started
Data importers for off-the-shelf devices
In active development; FitBit and Zeo currently work; BodyMedia and others on the way
Indoor microclimate station (Hardware; firmware in C)
Version 2 currently in alpha test; approaching beta-quality.
BodyTrack Chest strap (Hardware; firmware in C)
Version 3 currently in alpha test
Data importers for regional environmental data
In active development; many currently work
